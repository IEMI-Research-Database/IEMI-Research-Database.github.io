Abstract

Falsifying electrical signals in computer systemsâ€”the gateway between the physical and digital worldsâ€”intentional electromagnetic interference (IEMI) attacks have become increasingly pervasive and damaging to cyber-physical systems due to their ability to disrupt or control a wide range of safety- and security-critical applications. Existing studies of IEMI attacks are often highly device-specific and exploit disparate, insufficiently compared attack vectors. The absence of a unified, model-based understanding of IEMI vulnerabilities hinders both transferable security assessments and effective cross-disciplinary collaboration toward deployable protections. To address this gap, this work analyzes over 80 instances of IEMI attacks and defenses to provide an analytical framework that models how adversaries achieve IEMI coupling and sample manipulation to inject malicious electromagnetic energy that alters hardware behavior and impacts software execution. The primary goal is to move the field beyond exhaustive empirical discovery of vulnerable instances and toward in-depth theoretical analysis and proactive defense strategies applicable to both existing and future cyber-physical systems. In addition to identifying gaps in current IEMI attack and defense research, this work outlines important directions for future work tailored to the needs and roles of different stakeholder communities.

Motivation

Cyber-physical systems (CPS) underpin critical infrastructure including power grids, transportation, industrial control, and medical devices. These systems increasingly face electromagnetic interference threats. Intentional electromagnetic interference (IEMI) can lead to:

  • âœ— Sensor malfunctions or data tampering
  • âœ— Control signal disruption
  • âœ— System outages or unsafe operations

Despite growing IEMI threats, systematic study of CPS defenses remains limited. Our goal is to provide a comprehensive knowledge framework for researchers, engineers, and decision makers.

Attack Types

Major IEMI attack types covered include:

  • High-Power Microwave (HPM): Direct damage or interference to electronics
  • Electromagnetic Pulse (EMP): Induced currents and disruptions
  • Continuous Wave (CW) Jamming: Sustained interference of wireless links
  • Directed Energy Weapons (DEW): Precise, targeted attacks

Key Findings

1. Physical-Layer Vulnerabilities

CPS physical layers (sensors, networks) are widely susceptible to IEMI; many commercial sensors lack robust EMC design.

2. Defense Categories

Defenses can be grouped into three categories:

  • Passive protection: Shielding, isolation, grounding
  • Active protection: Detection and recovery algorithms
  • System-level protection: Redundancy and fault tolerance

3. Defense Gaps

Current defenses fall short in:

  • Lack of standardized security evaluation frameworks
  • Poor cross-domain coordination of defense strategies
  • Cost versus security trade-off challenges
